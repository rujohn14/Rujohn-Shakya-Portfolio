GorkhaSEC Research

Simulated Stress Analysis in Incident Response

Published · October 2025 · Western Sydney University

CAPSTONE RESEARCH

A research-driven capstone investigating how Security Operations Center automated pipelines perform under simultaneous, high-volume cyberattacks. Built a full virtual SOC environment and stress-tested incident response systems under realistic attack conditions.

Key Outcome

Concurrent attack campaigns measurably degraded automation. Triage latency increased by 40%, CPU utilisation peaked at 78%, and false positives nearly doubled. The team proposed the Stress-Resilient Incident Response (SRIR) framework.

CompanyGorkhaSEC
SupervisorRobert Beutel
InstitutionWestern Sydney University
Team4 Researchers

KEY RESEARCH FINDINGS

01

Automation Fragility Under Stress

40% increase in triage latency and near-double false positives during concurrent multi-vector attacks.

02

Hybrid Telemetry Scalability Limits

Process Explorer and PCAP produced high-fidelity forensic data but required manual correlation at scale.

03

Stealth Attack Detection Gaps

In-memory and non-persistent attacks evaded detection, showing the need for broader SOC telemetry.

04

SRIR Framework Proposed

Alert deduplication, adaptive correlation thresholds, load-aware automation tiers, and digital-twin rehearsal.
